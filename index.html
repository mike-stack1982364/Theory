The architecture

Ontological Worlds trains the construction of a world whose relations must remain valid locally, globally, generatively and across domains.

Relational integration is the combination of multiple relations into a unified, internally compatible model whose unstated implications, precise qualitative structure and possible transformations can be inferred.

The central unit of thought is not the isolated object. It is the object’s position within a system of relations, constraints, perspectives and possible transformations.

The six meta-operations

The first four are the bedrock of relational integration; the final two are its productive and generalising extensions.

Meta-mapping

Construct and compare multiple abstract mappings.

Meta-coherence

Test whether all mappings and generated details remain compatible.

Meta-logic

Infer relations and consequences not explicitly stated.

Meta-distinction

Resolve precise differences between neighbouring configurations.

Meta-generation

Create a context satisfying several constraints simultaneously.

Meta-transfer

Rebuild the same architecture through another domain.

Intellectual origin of the ontological categories

The category system has a precise authorship and inspiration chain. Preserving that distinction is essential to presenting the theory accurately.

Brandon Woodson: originator of the synthesis used here

In his 2 September 2014 essay, “A Correspondence of Ontological Categories and the Jewish Script”, Brandon Woodson explained that he had compared multiple proposed sets of basic ideas and found them either too general or incomplete, or too specific and superfluous.

Woodson then produced the distinctive nine-archetype system—repeated through archetypal, inner and outer forms—by studying Meru Foundation material, cross-checking other resources and refining the meanings into a practical analytic and cognitive-training taxonomy.

The functional definitions, mirrored pairings and proposed training use adopted here should therefore be attributed specifically to Brandon Woodson’s original synthesis.

Stan Tenen and Meru: principal creative scaffold

Woodson explicitly cited the Meru Research Foundation as a major resource. Meru’s research, led by Stan Tenen, organised the 27 Hebrew letter forms into a three-level “Matrix of Meaning”: Archetypal, Inner/Spiritual and Outer/Physical.

Tenen’s work treated meaning relationally—through position, symmetry, viewpoint, geometric metaphor and whole-system organisation. That architecture supplied the major structural and creative inspiration that Woodson transformed into his own category system.

Ontological categories: the operator grammar

Brandon Woodson’s nine-archetype synthesis, creatively inspired by Stan Tenen’s Meru Matrix of Meaning, becomes here a domain-independent grammar of relational operations.

Direction answers where. Category answers what operation. Perspective answers from which frame. Domain supplies the concrete content. Premises explain why the placement is warranted.

A category has no permanent direction, and a direction has no permanent semantic meaning.

All

Unity or common identity before differentiation.

Inner: whole · Outer: likeness · Archetypal: unifying identity

Difference

A boundary or contrast within a common field.

Inner: inside · Outer: outside · Archetypal: constituting distinction

Action

Change through agency or causal transformation.

Inner: action upon/within self · Outer: action toward another · Archetypal: actor–event–recipient

Division

Separation into independently tractable parts.

Inner: isolation · Outer: subdivision · Archetypal: unity becoming parts

Connection

The medium through which separated terms form a system.

Inner: hub · Outer: connected members · Archetypal: network relation

Multiplication / Unfoldment

Increase through reinforcement, growth or expansion.

Inner: strengthened capacity · Outer: proliferation · Archetypal: unit and field increased

Projection

A directed relation toward or away from a reference.

Inner: reception · Outer: emission · Archetypal: source–trajectory–destination

Encompassment

Containment in which boundary and contents co-determine.

Inner: boundary from within · Outer: enclosure by context · Archetypal: container relation

Completion

A configuration satisfying its relevant limits or fit.

Inner: sufficiency · Outer: bounded adequacy · Archetypal: neither deficient nor superfluous
All→Difference→Action→Division→Connection→Multiplication→Projection→Encompassment→Completion

Premise-derived topology

The compass is the visible result of relational integration, not the starting structure.

Example premises

T is north of J.

T = Jurassic Park
J = A typical zoo

L is east of J.

L = A biological laboratory

H lies between T and L.

H = Genetically engineered superhumans

What must be inferred

J is only a temporary reference node. The learner must infer what makes T northern, L eastern and H intermediate, then generate finer positions compatible with the complete model.

Triadic integration and recurrence

The category system becomes executable when category, perspective and topology must coexist through time.

Inner Action D — east to — Outer Multiplication J — south to — Projection M

The letters are temporary nodes; categories specify operations; perspectives specify orientation; directions specify relative topology. The learner must generate one world in which all constraints are true and infer the unstated relations among them.

Within-stream recombination

Integrates relations inside the current configuration.

Cross-trial recombination

Compares and revises structures retained from earlier configurations.

Cross-domain transformation

Cross-domain reconstruction tests whether the learner has abstracted the relation rather than memorised the example.

NNE — unprecedented creation

  • Biology: a new species.
  • Technology: a new form of computing.
  • Government: a previously nonexistent political form.
  • Language: a system for previously inexpressible relations.

ENE — intensive transformation

  • Biology: human-level cognition in primates.
  • Technology: narrow AI transformed into general reasoning.
  • Business: a conventional firm transformed through automation.
  • Education: an existing learner technologically augmented.

SW — compression for restoration

  • Biology: endangered life stored as genetic information.
  • Culture: a language archived for revival.
  • Technology: an ecosystem preserved as code and data.
  • Government: a collapsed state preserved through records.

WNW — systemic reconnection

  • Ecology: fragmented habitats reconnected.
  • Education: isolated disciplines integrated.
  • Psychology: compartmentalised memories reintegrated.
  • Technology: independent projects made interoperable.

A learner who remembers that Jurassic Park was north has learned a label. A learner who understands the premises that made it north can reconstruct an equivalent position through law, technology, medicine, politics, psychology or another domain.
