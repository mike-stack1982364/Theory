The architecture

Ontological Worlds trains the construction of a world whose relations must remain valid locally, globally, representationally, generatively and across domains.

Relational integration is the combination of multiple relations into a unified, internally compatible model whose unstated implications, precise qualitative structure and possible transformations can be inferred.

The central unit of thought is not the isolated object. It is the object’s position within a system of relations, constraints, perspectives and possible transformations.

The eight meta-operations

The additions are retained only because they identify operations with different objects, products and failure conditions.

Coordination

Meta-mapping

Constructs, aligns and compares mappings. Relational linking is a branch of this operation.

Representation

Meta-compression

Encodes relations among mappings into a denser form while preserving what is required for inference and reconstruction.

Validation

Meta-coherence

Tests whether mappings, compressions, premises and generated details remain mutually compatible.

Inference

Meta-logic

Derives consequences and relations not explicitly stated.

Resolution

Meta-distinction

Resolves the precise qualitative difference between competing structures.

Construction

Meta-synthesis

Forms a genuinely new higher-order whole from multiple abstract relational systems.

Instantiation

Meta-generation

Creates a concrete context satisfying several abstract constraints simultaneously.

Generalisation

Meta-transfer

Rebuilds the same relational architecture through another domain.

Recurrent control cycle

Map and link→Compress→Test coherence→Infer→Distinguish→Synthesise→Generate→Transfer→Revise

The sequence is not rigid. Failed transfer may expose a false abstraction; failed synthesis may reveal destructive compression; failed distinction may require remapping.

The redundancy boundary

Mapping, compression and synthesis are linked, but each operates on a different object.

The World Building Method

The accessible name for the constrained generative process previously called the Da Vinci Method and developed through Imagi-World.

A constrained generative mapping process in which the learner creates a new context whose concrete elements preserve an abstract relational structure and remain locally and globally compatible.

Meta-mapping establishes the field. Meta-compression makes the field manageable. Meta-synthesis forms the higher-order architecture. Meta-generation instantiates it as a world. Meta-coherence verifies the result.

Four validity standards

  1. Every immediate relation fits its premise.
  2. Neighbouring positions differ by the correct weighting.
  3. No example contradicts the global topology.
  4. The same structure survives cross-domain reconstruction.

Novelty without arbitrariness

Examples should resist rote memorisation while remaining objectively constrained. Novelty is an anti-strategy design condition, not proof of cognitive transfer.

Spark → riot: local physical ignition becomes cascading social combustion. The content changes; the causal-relational vector remains.

Intellectual origin of the categories

The authorship and inspiration chain is kept explicit so that structural influence, original synthesis and later operational development are not conflated.

Brandon Woodson: originator of the synthesis used here

In his 2 September 2014 essay, “A Correspondence of Ontological Categories and the Jewish Script”, Woodson described comparing candidate sets of fundamental ideas before developing a nine-archetype scheme repeated through archetypal, inner and outer forms.

The functional definitions, mirrored pairings and proposed cognitive-training use adopted here should therefore be attributed specifically to Brandon Woodson’s original synthesis.

Stan Tenen and Meru: principal creative scaffold

Woodson explicitly cited Meru Foundation material as a major resource. Meru research led by Stan Tenen organised 27 Hebrew letter forms into Archetypal, Inner/Spiritual and Outer/Physical levels and treated meaning through position, symmetry, perspective and whole-system organisation.

Tenen supplied a principal structural and creative inspiration, not Woodson’s exact taxonomy.

Ontological categories: the operator grammar

The categories answer what operation is occurring. They do not permanently define a compass direction.

Direction answers where. Category answers what operation. Perspective answers from which frame. Domain supplies concrete content. Premises explain why the placement is warranted.

All

Unity or common identity before differentiation.

Inner: whole · Outer: likeness · Archetypal: unifying identity

Difference

A boundary or contrast within a common field.

Inner: inside · Outer: outside · Archetypal: constituting distinction

Action

Change through agency or causal transformation.

Inner: within self · Outer: toward another · Archetypal: actor–event–recipient

Division

Separation into independently tractable parts.

Inner: isolation · Outer: subdivision · Archetypal: unity becoming parts

Connection

The medium through which separated terms form a system.

Inner: hub · Outer: connected members · Archetypal: network relation

Multiplication / Unfoldment

Increase through reinforcement, growth or expansion.

Inner: strengthened capacity · Outer: proliferation · Archetypal: increased unit and field

Projection

A directed relation toward or away from a reference.

Inner: reception · Outer: emission · Archetypal: source–trajectory–destination

Encompassment

Containment in which boundary and contents co-determine.

Inner: boundary from within · Outer: enclosure · Archetypal: container relation

Completion

A configuration satisfying its relevant limits or fit.

Inner: sufficiency · Outer: bounded adequacy · Archetypal: exact completion
All→Difference→Action→Division→Connection→Multiplication→Projection→Encompassment→Completion

Category affinities with meta-compression

Division separates relevant from irrelevant structure; Connection preserves dependencies; Completion expresses the smallest sufficient representation. These are affinities, not fixed equivalences.

Category affinities with meta-synthesis

All, Connection, Multiplication and Encompassment frequently organise several mappings into a new whole. Again, the category grammar and meta-operation remain distinct levels.

Triadic integration and recurrence

The system becomes executable when category, perspective, topology, compression and synthesis must coexist through time.

Inner Action D — east to — Outer Multiplication J — south to — Projection M

The learner maps and links the relations, compresses the configuration into a manipulable model, tests coherence, infers unstated relations, synthesises the constraints into a unified organisation and generates one concrete world in which every relation is true.

Within-stream recombination

Integrates relations in the current configuration.

Cross-trial recombination

Compares and revises structures retained from earlier configurations.

Cross-domain transformation

Transfer tests whether the learner captured the invariant relation rather than memorised its surface expression.

NNE — unprecedented creation

  • Biology: a new species.
  • Technology: a new form of computing.
  • Government: a previously nonexistent political form.
  • Language: a system for previously inexpressible relations.

ENE — intensive transformation

  • Biology: human-level cognition in primates.
  • Technology: narrow AI transformed into general reasoning.
  • Business: a conventional firm transformed through automation.
  • Education: an existing learner technologically augmented.

SW — compression for restoration

  • Biology: endangered life stored as genetic information.
  • Culture: an endangered language archived for revival.
  • Technology: a discontinued ecosystem preserved as code and data.
  • Government: a collapsed state preserved through records.

WNW — systemic reconnection

  • Ecology: fragmented habitats reconnected.
  • Education: isolated disciplines integrated.
  • Psychology: compartmentalised memories reintegrated.
  • Technology: independent projects made interoperable.

Cross-domain work requires the learner to map the correspondence, compress the invariant relation, synthesise it with the constraints of the new domain and generate a coherent new expression.
