The architecture

Ontological Worlds trains the construction of a world whose relations must remain valid locally, globally, generatively and across domains.

Relational integration is the combination of multiple relations into a unified, internally compatible model whose unstated implications, precise qualitative structure and possible transformations can be inferred.

The central unit of thought is not the isolated object. It is the object’s position within a system of relations, constraints, perspectives and possible transformations.

The six meta-operations

The first four are the bedrock of relational integration; the final two are its productive and generalising extensions.

Meta-mapping

Construct, hold, align and compare multiple abstract mappings.

Meta-coherence

Test whether all mappings and generated details remain compatible.

Meta-logic

Infer relations and consequences not explicitly stated.

Meta-distinction

Resolve precise differences between neighbouring configurations.

Meta-generation

Create a context satisfying several constraints simultaneously.

Meta-transfer

Rebuild the same architecture through another domain.

Map→Coherence→Infer→Distinguish→Generate→Transfer→Revise

The World Building Method

The accessible name for the generative process previously called the Da Vinci Method and developed through Imagi-World.

A constrained generative mapping process in which the learner creates a new context whose concrete elements preserve an abstract relational structure and remain locally and globally compatible.

Association supplies the nodes; relational binding supplies the connections; the ontological categories supply the operator grammar; topology supplies relative placement; and the meta-operations verify the completed world.

Validity conditions

  1. Every immediate relation fits its premise.
  2. Near neighbours differ by the correct weighting.
  3. No example contradicts the global topology.
  4. The structure survives cross-domain reconstruction.

Novelty without arbitrariness

Examples should resist rote memorisation while remaining objectively constrained. Novelty is an anti-strategy design condition, not proof of cognitive transfer.

Spark → riot: local physical ignition becomes cascading social combustion. The content changes; the causal-relational vector remains.

Ontological categories: the operator grammar

Nine archetypes, each represented through inner, outer and archetypal perspectives, produce 27 usable operator forms.

Direction answers where. Category answers what operation. Perspective answers from which frame. Domain supplies the concrete content. Premises explain why the placement is warranted.

A category has no permanent direction, and a direction has no permanent semantic meaning. This separation lets the same operator set organise unlimited worlds.

All

Unity or common identity before differentiation.

Inner
The whole apprehended as one.
Outer
Likenesses or repeated instances.
Archetypal
The unity joining whole and likeness.

Difference

A boundary or contrast within a common field.

Inner
What belongs inside the frame.
Outer
What lies outside it.
Archetypal
The distinction constituting both.

Action

Change through agency or causal transformation.

Inner
Action upon or within the self.
Outer
Action directed toward another.
Archetypal
Actor, transformation and recipient.

Division

Separation into independently tractable parts.

Inner
Self-removal or isolation.
Outer
Subdivision or pluralisation.
Archetypal
Unity becoming differentiated parts.

Connection

The medium through which separated terms form a system.

Inner
The hub or interface.
Outer
The connected members.
Archetypal
The network relation itself.

Multiplication / Unfoldment

Increase through reinforcement, growth or expansion.

Inner
Increased internal capacity.
Outer
Proliferation or outward growth.
Archetypal
Strengthened unit and expanded field.

Projection

A directed relation toward or away from a reference.

Inner
Reception or convergence.
Outer
Emission or extension.
Archetypal
Source, trajectory and destination.

Encompassment

Containment in which boundary and contents co-determine.

Inner
The boundary experienced within.
Outer
Enclosure by context.
Archetypal
Container, boundary and contents.

Completion

A configuration satisfying its relevant limits or fit.

Inner
Internal sufficiency.
Outer
Adequacy without excess.
Archetypal
Neither deficient nor superfluous.

Premise-derived topology

The compass is the visible result of relational integration, not the starting structure.

Example premises

T is north of J.

T = Jurassic Park
J = A typical zoo

L is east of J.

L = A biological laboratory

H lies between T and L.

H = Genetically engineered superhumans

What is inferred

J is only a temporary reference node. The learner must infer what makes T northern, L eastern and H intermediate, then generate finer positions compatible with the entire model.

Triadic integration and recurrence

The categories become an executable grammar when several category–perspective–topology constraints must coexist through time.

Inner Action D — east to — Outer Multiplication J — south to — Projection M

The letters are temporary nodes; categories specify operations; perspectives specify orientation; directions specify relative topology. The learner must generate one context in which all constraints are true and infer the unstated relations among them.

Within-stream recombination

Integrates relations inside the current configuration.

Cross-trial recombination

Compares and revises structures retained from earlier configurations.

Research caution: timing and load require calibration. Extreme speed or load should not be represented as established evidence of broad cognitive improvement.

Cross-domain transformation

Cross-domain reconstruction is the principal test of whether the learner has abstracted the relation rather than memorised an example.

NNE — unprecedented creation

  • Biology: a new species.
  • Technology: a new form of computing.
  • Government: a previously nonexistent political form.
  • Language: a system for previously inexpressible relations.

ENE — intensive transformation

  • Biology: human-level cognition in primates.
  • Technology: narrow AI transformed into general reasoning.
  • Business: a conventional firm transformed through automation.
  • Education: an existing learner technologically augmented.

SW — compression for restoration

  • Biology: endangered life stored as genetic information.
  • Culture: an endangered language archived for revival.
  • Technology: a discontinued ecosystem preserved as code and data.
  • Government: a collapsed state preserved through records.

WNW — systemic reconnection

  • Ecology: fragmented habitats reconnected.
  • Education: isolated disciplines integrated.
  • Psychology: compartmentalised memories reintegrated.
  • Technology: independent projects made interoperable.

A learner who remembers that Jurassic Park was north has learned a label. A learner who understands the premises that made it north can reconstruct an equivalent position through law, technology, medicine, politics, psychology or another domain.

Research status and testable claims

The framework is conceptually integrated, but conceptual integration is not proof of increased general intelligence or far transfer.

Novel relational inferenceAccuracy on unseen structures.
Near-neighbour discriminationAccuracy among closely weighted alternatives.
Global-coherence errorLocally plausible answers that contradict the whole.
Generative validityWhether generated contexts satisfy all constraints.
Cross-domain transferStructure preserved when domain and vocabulary change.
Temporal revisionUpdating the model when later premises change implications.

The categories supply the operator grammar. The World Building Method supplies generative execution. Directional topology supplies relational resolution. Temporal recurrence keeps the model active and revisable through time.
